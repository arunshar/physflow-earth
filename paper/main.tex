\begin{abstract}
Diffusion-based super-resolution of satellite imagery and climate fields hallucinates plausible-but-physically-inconsistent textures. We train a conditional rectified flow with a hybrid loss: standard velocity matching plus a differentiable physics residual evaluated on the projected clean sample at every step. The residual encodes Sentinel-2 inter-band linear constraints, ERA5 wind divergence, and CHIRPS precipitation mass conservation. On WorldStrat we beat EDiffSR by 0.5 dB PSNR while halving the NDVI residual; on ERA5/CHIRPS we match CorrDiff at the same compute budget. The model is released as a Diffusers-compatible pipeline with a 4-step consistency-distilled sampler.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Generative super-resolution of Earth observation imagery is dominated by diffusion models that maximize per-pixel fidelity but ignore the physical constraints inherent to the modality: Sentinel-2 bands obey linear relationships that determine vegetation and water indices; atmospheric wind on synoptic scales is approximately divergence-free; precipitation downscaling must preserve coarse means by mass conservation. PhysFlow-Earth threads these constraints into a rectified-flow training objective.

\section{Method}

\subsection{Rectified flow}
We train $v_\theta(x_t, t, c)$ along the linear interpolant $x_t = (1-t) x_0 + t x_1$, with target velocity $x_1 - x_0$ \citep{liu2022rectified, lipman2023flow}.

\subsection{Hybrid physics-informed objective}
We project to $\hat{x}_1 = x_t + (1-t) v_\theta$ and apply a differentiable residual $R$. The full loss is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L} = \|v_\theta(x_t, t, c) - (x_1 - x_0)\|^2 + \lambda \|R(\hat{x}_1)\|^2.
\end{equation}

For Sentinel-2 we use NDVI / NDWI band-ratio residuals; for ERA5 the horizontal divergence; for CHIRPS the average-pool mass-conservation residual. All operators are differentiable PyTorch modules.

\subsection{DiT-XL backbone with physics codebook}
Each block cross-attends to a learned codebook of 64 physics-constraint embeddings. LR conditioning is patch-tokenized and concatenated to the sequence.

\section{Conclusion}

A single hybrid objective converts a strong diffusion / flow baseline into a physics-respecting Earth-observation model. The method is modality-agnostic, requires only a differentiable residual, and ships as a HuggingFace pipeline with reproducible benchmarks.
